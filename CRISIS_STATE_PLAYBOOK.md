# CRISIS STATE PLAYBOOK: Girl Secondary Dropout Intervention Framework

**For:** District Collectors, State Education Secretaries (IAS officers)  
**Use:** Apply this framework to your state if secondary girls' dropout > 15%

---

## QUICK DIAGNOSIS (Do This First — 1 hour)

**Your state is in crisis-intervention zone if:**
- Current secondary girls' dropout > 15% (national average: 12.46%)
- **OR** visible drift upward in last 2 years
- **OR** 3+ districts with >25% dropout

### Immediate Questions to Ask
```
1. What is our current secondary girls' dropout rate? ____%
2. Has it worsened in the last 2 years? YES / NO
3. Which 5-10 districts have the worst rates? ___________________

   ☐ Social (caste, community opposition, marriage)
   ☐ Physical (distance to school, no girls' schools nearby)
   ☐ Quality (unsafe schools, poor teaching, irrelevant curriculum)
   ☐ Other: _________________
```

## THE PROVEN PLAYBOOK (4-Step Intervention Template)

### STEP 1: ECONOMIC SUPPORT — Direct to Target Girls
**Fastest intervention, measurable impact**

**What works (evidence from Tripura, Manipur, MP):**
- ₹500-1000/month scholarship for girls in Class IX-X in dropout-risk families
- Disbursed directly to girl or mother (not school)
- Timing critical: Aug-Nov each year (when dropout spike happens)
- Condition: Enrollment + attendance ≥80%

**Implementation checklist:**
- [ ] Identify dropout-risk girls (poor, SC/ST, far from school, first-generation learners)
- [ ] Partner with NRLM/ICDS for identification + verification
- [ ] Set up direct bank transfer process (no cash, no intermediaries)
- [ ] Align with existing state schemes (MGSY, KANYA, etc.) — don't duplicate
- [ ] Track: Monthly attendance + scholarship disbursement (RAG status)

**Expected impact:** 3-5% immediate dropout reduction (best case: -8 to -10%)  
**Timeline:** Full rollout in 3-4 months  
**Cost:** ₹50-100 cr/year for 500K girls

---

### STEP 2: INFRASTRUCTURE + SAFETY — Build Trust
**Takes 6-12 months; sustains long-term change**

**What works (evidence from Himachal, Sikkim):**
- Separate girls' toilets (single most-cited barrier in research)
- Safe transportation (school buses, transport passes, safe routes)
- Girls' hostel/day-care near secondary schools (for rural communities)
- Security measures (CCTV, female staff, grievance mechanism)

**Implementation checklist:**
- [ ] Survey: Ask girls and parents — what's the #1 reason to drop out? (Physical safety is #1 in most crisis states)
- [ ] Quick-win: Install girls' toilets in top 50 dropout-risk schools within 6 months
- [ ] Medium-term: Evaluate transport + hostel needs; plan FY2027-28 CAPEX
- [ ] Establish girls' grievance cell in every district (anonymous, phone-based OK)

**Expected impact:** 2-3% additional reduction + sustained retention  
**Timeline:** Prioritize top 30% of schools first; full rollout in 18 months  
**Cost:** ₹30-50 cr/year (CAPEX); ₹5-10 cr/year (operations)

---

### STEP 3: SOCIAL MOBILIZATION — Change Narrative
**Lowest cost; most sustainable impact**

**What works (evidence from Manipur, Madhya Pradesh):**
- Village education committees (fathers + mothers + teachers) meet monthly
- Focus: Why girls' education matters (economic + social)
- Targeted outreach: Reach families who withdrew girls last year
- Male engagement: Fathers/brothers as champions (not just mothers)

**Implementation checklist:**
- [ ] Form "Girl Education Committee" in every village with >20% dropout (1 meeting/month)
- [ ] Recruit female peer educators (Class XII pass-outs, incentivized)
- [ ] Track: % girls re-enrolled from last-year dropout + first-term attendance
- [ ] Celebrate: Public recognition (diploma, small cash award) for villages achieving 3-year zero-dropout
- [ ] Media: Radio + local WhatsApp groups with girl success stories

**Expected impact:** 2-4% reduction + community ownership  
**Timeline:** Launch within 2 months; measure results at term-end  
**Cost:** ₹2-5 cr/year (incentives + meetings)

---

### STEP 4: TEACHER + QUALITY — Sustain Progress
**Medium-term; essential for preventing relapse**

**What works (evidence from SATH program, Odisha pilots):**
- Training: Math/English teachers on pedagogy for struggling girls (not advanced content)
- Mentorship: Assign one teacher as girls' academic mentor (grades 9-10)
- Curriculum: Clarify relevance (vocational pathways, exam link, career options)
- Feedback: Monthly tracking of mid-term/month-end scores — flag at-risk girls early

**Implementation checklist:**
- [ ] Conduct mini-surveys: Ask girls why they find secondary hard (math? peer pressure? relevance?)
- [ ] Prioritize: Intensive teacher training for math/English in top 30 schools
- [ ] Early warning: Set up system to flag girls scoring <40% in any month
- [ ] Catch-up: Organize 10-hour summer bridge camps (June-July) for at-risk girls

**Expected impact:** 1-2% additional reduction; sustained 2-3 year gains  
**Timeline:** Q2 teacher training; Q3 mentorship + tracking  
**Cost:** ₹5-10 cr/year

## COMMON PITFALLS (Avoid These)

❌ **Scholarship without attendance tracking** — Money wasted, girls still drop out  
✅ Do: Link disbursement to verified attendance

❌ **Toilets without maintenance plan** — Broken in 6 months  
✅ Do: Assign maintenance budget + female keeper from village

❌ **Committees that don't meet or measure** — Theater, not change  
✅ Do: Score committees on tangible re-enrollment metrics

❌ **Top-down teacher training without classroom follow-up** — Forgotten in 3 weeks  
✅ Do: Monthly peer teaching circles where teachers demo in each other's classes
