# GIRLS' SECONDARY EDUCATION: ONE-PAGE POLICY BRIEF

**For:** Ministry of Education Leadership  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Data Source:** UDISE+ (2015-22)  
**Status:** India is **on track** to achieve single-digit girls' secondary dropout (9.17%) by academic year 2024-25—but critical state interventions are needed now to sustain momentum.

---

## THE HEADLINE

**India improved girls' secondary dropout from 18.26% to 12.46% in 7 years (32% reduction). At current trajectory (−1.40 pp/year), reaches 9.17% by academic year 2024-25. But five states still lose >20% of girls—requiring immediate, targeted action.**

---

## KEY FINDINGS (60 seconds)

| Finding | Data | Implication |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Progress is real** | -1.4 pp/year consistent, 7-year trend | National policies working; maintain investment |
| **Inequality is extreme** | 0% (Chandigarh) vs 25.3% (Odisha) | Crisis states need 4-5x resource allocation |
| **Rapid turnarounds possible** | Tripura: 45.7% → 12.5% in 7 years | Success model exists; can be replicated |
| **Secondary transition is critical** | 12% dropout only at Class IX→X; <2% elsewhere | Policy ROI highest at transition point |

---

## THREE IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

### 1️⃣ **INTERVENTIONS in Crisis States (Odisha, Bihar, Meghalaya, Assam, West Bengal)**
   - **What:** Replicate Tripura/Manipur models (economic support + social mobilization)
   - **Where:** Focus on 50-100 highest-dropout districts
   - **Cost:** ~₹300-400 cr/year (scholarship + infrastructure)
   - **Timeline:** Full implementation by April 2027
   - **Target:** Reduce crisis state average from 21.8% to 14% within 18 months

### 2️⃣ **PEER LEARNING NETWORKS**
   - **Connect:** Every high-dropout district with a peer success district
   - **Mechanism:** Bi-monthly cross-state officer exchanges, practice documentation
   - **Example:** Bihar officers visit Manipur; learn implementation, adapt locally
   - **Cost:** ~₹20 cr/year (travel + coordination)
   - **Gain:** Compressed learning cycle; 6-month feedback loop

### 3️⃣ **MEASUREMENT & EARLY WARNING**
   - **Track:** Monthly district-level data on transition-stage girls' enrollment
   - **Alert:** Automatic flag if any district shows >2% month-on-month dropout uptick
   - **Response:** RAG-coded dashboard for state education secretaries (Red=crisis, Amber=watch, Green=tracking)
   - **Cost:** ~₹5 cr/year (data infrastructure)
   - **Value:** Early intervention before cohorts are lost

## SUCCESS EVIDENCE

### Why We Know This Works

| State | Model | Result |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Tripura** | Conditional cash transfer + community mobilization | **-33.3 pp** (45.7% → 12.5%) |
| **Manipur** | Social capital + targeted scholarship | **-14.4 pp** (15.7% → 1.3%) |
| **Madhya Pradesh** | Infrastructure + teacher training | **-23.2 pp** (31.9% → 8.6%) |
| **Himachal Pradesh** | Physical accessibility + no-cost schemes | Stable at **0.91%** |

**Common elements:** Economic support + Trust-building + Community engagement + Physical infrastructure
