2022-23: Brief recovery to 10.6%

The pattern is clear: we can't sustain improvement. Recovery bounces back to baseline.

Regional Hotspots: Where the Crisis is Worst

Dropout isn't distributed evenly. It clusters. Take the Northeast — a 5-state block (Assam, Arunachal, Meghalaya, Manipur, Mizoram) — and you have nearly 20% dropout rates. Meanwhile, Southern states average 7-8%.

What We Know & What It Means

Finding 1: The Barrier is NOT Always Poverty

West Bengal and Assam have reasonably developed infrastructure. Yet they have crisis-level dropouts. The barrier is something else — early marriage, distance to school, safety concerns.

Finding 2: One Solution Doesn't Fit All

There are two regional crisis patterns: (1) Northeast — uniform infrastructure crisis affecting BOTH genders equally. (2) Central/South — gender-differentiated crisis, boys at-risk more.

Finding 3: The Exemplar Model Works

States that combine female teacher recruitment + toilet enforcement + conditional cash transfer see 3-5pp dropout reductions within 18-24 months. It's replicable.

Finding 4: The Gender Reversal is Real

Boys drop out more in 35/36 states. This isn't noise. This means policies assuming "girls are at-risk" are outdated. We need gender-aware (not gender-neutral) policy that addresses both.

Bottom line: We have the solutions. They're demonstrated in exemplar states. The question is whether we'll scale them or keep discussing the problem.
